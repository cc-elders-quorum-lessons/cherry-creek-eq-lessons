Do Your Part with All Your Heart

Dieter F. Uchtdorf · Priesthood circle lesson on spiritual gifts, honesty, engagement, and priesthood action.

Read Talk Watch Video

Core Scriptures

Psalm 147:3 Luke 22:32 Mosiah 18:8–9 Ether 12:27

Weekly Reflection

  • Counsel with your wife and pray together.
  • Talk with your children about the hardest part of their week.
  • Give or request a priesthood blessing.
  • Minister to one family with prayer, presence, or scripture.
  • Practice one small technology fast.

Highlighted Closing

If we do our part with all our heart, God will do His part with all His power—and He will make more of us than we could ever make of ourselves.

Lesson Flow

  1. Set the room in a circle and begin with a brief calming exercise.
  2. Phones on the floor, faces up, attention on each other.
  3. Use the “Broncos” moment as a bridge into the question of where men place energy and excitement.
  4. Discuss how Jesus taught real people in real places without waiting for perfection.
  5. Invite the quorum to name burdens men carry silently and gifts they bury.
  6. Teach that priesthood power grows through action, humility, and trying.
  7. Close with the ripple image: one small priesthood act can bless generations.

Questions for the Circle

  • What part of your spiritual life gets pushed aside by technology?
  • Why do men hide instead of opening up?
  • If Christ walked into the room right now, what would He want this quorum to become?
  • What wisdom do younger men need from older men?
  • What one small spiritual action would change your week?

December 14, 2025

The Lord Looketh on the Heart

Ronald M. Barcellos · A lesson centered on quiet miracles, real connection, and how Christ works through ordinary moments.

Read Talk Watch Video

Teaching Notes

  • Start with a clean, disarming opening that shifts attention away from the teacher and onto Christ.
  • Frame the lesson around quiet ministering, human connection, and the Lord working in ordinary moments.
  • Use the story only as a bridge to doctrine, not as the center of the lesson.

Suggested Opening

This lesson isn’t about me. It’s about recognizing how Christ works with all of us.

Related Reading

A Christmas-season Substack story about unexpected connection, laughter, healing, and being reminded that the Lord still reaches His children quietly through ordinary people.

Lesson Structure

  1. God works quietly through ordinary people.
  2. Presence is a form of ministering.
  3. Our mission is not over just because life becomes painful or difficult.
  4. The Savior still heals hearts through connection, kindness, and quiet moments of grace.

Discussion Questions

  • When have you felt the Lord speak softly instead of dramatically?
  • Who in your life needs your presence more than your advice?
  • How do we sometimes decide too early that our season of usefulness is over?
  • What quiet form of healing has the Savior given you that was not physical, but still real?

The Family Proclamation—Words from God

Ronald A. Rasband · A family-centered lesson on revelation, identity, discouragement, engagement, and the work of priesthood in real homes.

Read Talk Watch Video

Core Scriptures

Genesis 1:27 2 Corinthians 12:9 Mosiah 18:8–9 John 5:8–9 Philippians 4:13 Doctrine and Covenants 50:24

Anchor Truth

The Family Proclamation is not a checklist for perfect people. It is God’s rescue plan for families led by imperfect men who keep showing up.

Lesson Flow

  1. Set confidentiality and trust as part of quorum culture.
  2. Anchor the lesson in Elder Rasband’s teaching that the proclamation is revelation.
  3. Ask what makes revealed family doctrine hard to live in real life.
  4. Discuss discouragement, exhaustion, and mental strain without shame.
  5. Teach that diagnosis may explain pain, but it must not replace eternal identity.
  6. Invite one act of engagement this week: prayer, service, conversation, or re-connection.

Discussion Questions

  • What does it mean to you personally that the proclamation contains words from God?
  • What makes it hard to live revealed family doctrine in daily life?
  • How does discouragement show up in ordinary family patterns?
  • What one act this week would interrupt isolation in your home?

January 11, 2026

Remembering the Sheep

William K. Jackson · Brotherhood, presence, shepherding, and the power of personal connection in a distracted age.

Read Talk Watch Video

Scripture Study

Moroni 6:4–5 Moroni 7:45–47 3 Nephi 17:7–9 3 Nephi 18:31–32 Luke 15:4–7 John 10:14–15 James 5:16

Weekly Challenge

Write down one person you feel prompted to shepherd better, and then name one face-to-face action you will take this week.

Lesson Flow

  1. Open with the difference between checking boxes and treating people like souls.
  2. Compare Moroni’s church culture with modern busy and distracted life.
  3. Teach that Christ did not lead from a distance; He led through relationships.
  4. Contrast community built on presence with community built on isolation and scrolling.
  5. Use the shepherd passages to emphasize movement, initiative, and staying with the one.
  6. Close with personal action instead of abstract agreement.

Questions for the Quorum

  • What does it really mean to know your sheep?
  • Does faster always mean better when it comes to people?
  • What happens when charity is missing and comparison grows?
  • What effort does the shepherd make for the one?
  • Why does healing often require connection?

January 25, 2026

The Atoning Love of Jesus Christ

Neil L. Andersen · Repentance, healing, radical honesty, drift, family confidence, and the Savior’s grace in real life.

Read Talk Watch Video

Scripture Study

Mosiah 27 Isaiah 1:18 Enos 1:2–9 Doctrine and Covenants 58:42–43 Alma 17:2–3 3 Nephi 9:13

Strong Line

Nothing on a screen is worth losing our light.

Buddy Check

Each man chooses one person to serve this week and one buddy for a midweek text or check-in.

Lesson Flow

  1. Frame repentance as a heavenly tutorial rather than a courtroom.
  2. Use check-in rounds to open the circle with honesty and without pressure.
  3. Discuss complete honesty with God, self, and priesthood leaders.
  4. Teach safe, trusted priesthood leadership in the home.
  5. Address digital drift, quiet habits, and things that slowly take light from families.
  6. Testify that the Savior heals both the sinner who returns and the wounded who have been hurt by others.

Questions for Discussion

  • How does calling repentance a tutorial change your willingness to be honest?
  • What weight could honesty help you let go of?
  • How can fathers and husbands become safe harbors in a digital age?
  • How do we stay faithful in the “middle part” when relief comes slowly?

February 8, 2026

And Now I See

Jeffrey R. Holland · Testimony as lived evidence, small and simple discipleship, priesthood leadership through love, and the power of simply showing up.

Read Talk Watch Video

Scripture Study

John 9 Joshua 24:14–15 Doctrine and Covenants 121:41–45 Alma 37:6–7 Matthew 9:35–36 Matthew 5:14–16 Mosiah 18:8–9 Doctrine and Covenants 84:88

Lesson Flow

  1. Set the tone: this quorum is not about impressing anyone.
  2. Use Elder Holland’s title to explore what it means to say, “Now I see.”
  3. Teach from John 9 that testimony is lived evidence, not argument.
  4. Use Alma 37 to show that miracles often arrive through small and simple means.
  5. Bear witness that faith often steadies men long before it explains everything.
  6. Close by asking who needs us to truly see them this week.

Questions for Discussion

  • What does “I see” mean to you spiritually?
  • If someone watched your life this week, what evidence would they see of Christ?
  • What small and simple act mattered most in your life?
  • What does real priesthood leadership look like at home?
  • Who needs you to really see them this week?

The Power of Ministering to the One

Peter M. Johnson · Ministering, real contact, modern tools, real presence, and leading souls toward Jesus Christ and the house of the Lord.

Read Talk Watch Video Related Video

Scripture Study

Luke 15:4–7 John 4:7–14 3 Nephi 11:14–15 Mosiah 18:8–9 Helaman 5:12 Doctrine and Covenants 20:53–55 Doctrine and Covenants 6:21–23

Memorable Lines

Technology didn’t weaken ministering. Comfort did.
A text can open a door. But it can’t replace walking through it.

Lesson Flow

  1. Set the quorum as a brotherhood where every voice matters.
  2. Ask who “the one” is and invite men to remember what it feels like to be unseen.
  3. Use a simple round-the-circle answer so every man speaks at least once.
  4. Teach that ministering is not a checked box, but a relationship that leads toward Christ.
  5. Use John 4 and the old home teaching clip to compare contact with real presence.
  6. Invite one act of contact this week that moves toward real relationship.

Discussion Questions

  • Who comes to mind when you hear “the one”?
  • What quality do you most appreciate in someone who has ministered to you?
  • What happens when ministering becomes an assignment instead of a relationship?
  • What makes ministering hard right now—and what would help you do it better?

March 8, 2026

Proved and Strengthened in Christ

Henry B. Eyring · Eternal perspective, trials, brotherhood, Christ as foundation, and the Lord’s power to strengthen willing disciples.

Read Talk Watch Video

Scripture Study

Abraham 3:24–25 Ether 12:6–7 Helaman 5:12 Mosiah 18:8–9 Doctrine and Covenants 121:7–8 Romans 5:3–5

Opening Perspective

Use eternal perspective as a frame: gifts can be refined through trial, beautiful things can come out of suffering, and men grow stronger when they see their lives in the larger plan of salvation.

Lesson Flow

  1. Begin by asking what helps a group of men grow stronger while keeping Christ at the center.
  2. Teach from Abraham 3 that being proved is part of the plan from the beginning.
  3. Use Ether 12 to teach faith before the witness.
  4. Anchor the quorum on Helaman 5:12 and Christ as foundation.
  5. Invite a reflection pause: what is proving your faith right now?
  6. Show how trials can prepare disciples to comfort and strengthen others.
  7. Close by testifying that willing service matters more than polish or appearance.

Discussion Questions

  • Why do you think proving is part of God’s plan instead of life simply being easy?
  • Why does the witness often come after the trial?
  • What does it mean in practical terms to build on Christ?
  • How do our own trials prepare us to help other people later?
  • How can difficult experiences eventually lead to hope?

Future Update

Weekly Men’s Group proposal

An upcoming possibility for Wednesday-night follow-up discussion, brotherhood, and support outside of Sunday quorum.

Purpose

A weekly place for men to talk honestly, support each other, and strengthen Christ-centered homes. The goal is brotherhood, not performance.

Possible Focus Areas

  • Faith at home and family leadership
  • Technology drift and screen distraction
  • Isolation, discouragement, and pressure carried in silence
  • Brotherhood, practical service, and real conversation
  • Helping fathers, husbands, and priesthood holders reconnect at home

Simple Framework

  1. Open with prayer and a short check-in round.
  2. Use a simple weekly prompt to get men talking.
  3. Keep the circle focused on real life, faith, and support.
  4. Use scriptures or a short Church-based framework as needed.
  5. Close with one practical action for the week.
